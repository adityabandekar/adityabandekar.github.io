Who is most responsible for the climate crisis?

In Assignment 2, I created a zine which asked this question. I showed multiple visualizations which argue different points of views. Here I extend those visualizations to be interactive.

One question we can ask is which countries pollute the most CO2 by sheer number.

CO2 emissions by country

Drag the timeline below to zoom. Double-click to reset.

We see that China, the United States, and India produce the most CO2. Furthermore, 6 of the top 10 countries are from the Asian continent. On first glance, this implies that these countries are most the responsible countries in pollution.

Let's now show this on a map to make it clear which countries pollute the most.

CO2 emissions by country


We see that larger countries pollute the most. However, this feels like an unfair comparison because each country has a different population. For example European pollution is distributed among multiple smaller countries, which make their impact seem smaller.

So maybe instead we should ask which countries pollute the most per capita?

CO2 emissions by country per capita


Now we see that China and India produce less CO2 emissions per capita than a lot of other countries. Here it seems that most developed countries are producing similar amounts of CO2 emissions.

European countries produce less CO2 emissions per capita than USA and China and are declining in emissions per capita. Next, a few nations in the middle east which have a lot of oil resources are producing a lot of CO2 emissions per capita.

In conclusion, based on how we visualize the data, we can draw different conclusions.
